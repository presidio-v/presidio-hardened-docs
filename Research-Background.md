## Research paradigm

Computational jurisprudence (CJ) — the discipline in which compliance becomes a runtime, compositional, proof-carrying property of computation itself rather than documentation checked at human latency — is the paradigm that organises Stantchev's current research. Its lineage runs through almost three decades of his work: architectural translucency and dependable service-oriented computing with Malek (nonfunctional properties as first-class, machine-checkable concerns), QoS and SLA negotiation and enforcement in grid and cloud settings (2009), IT and cloud governance, GRC and ISMS process frameworks (2014–2019), standards-and-risk requirements engineering for blockchain (2020), and fog/edge architectures where authorization must hold locally and offline. The x402 PII-safe agentic-payments work ([arXiv 2604.11430](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11430)) and the CJ position paper ([doi:10.5281/zenodo.21245659](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.21245659)) make the through-line explicit: authority that only attenuates as it delegates, verification that is local and fail-closed with no engine in the trust path, and evidence that composes.
